International Football Results Questions:

  1. Your boss wants to know the number of football games by year. You should show at minimum 5 years, but you can choose which years to show.

 

-My first visualization shown is a bar plot that displays the number of games from 2015 to 2020. I did this by grouping data by year and summing each occurrence of a game in a given year (the preprocessing of the data can be found in make_csv.py in the data folder.  The height of the bars represent the given amount of games for the year denoted on the x-axis. 

 

 

  1. Your boss wants to understand the top winning nations. We would like to see a winning percentage for the top 10 nations. You can show this in a map form if you would like to.

 

-My third visualization displays a world map in which the top ten most winning nations are denoted in blue. If you hover your cursor over a given country it’s winning ratio will be displayed to the left of the map if there is data on it. To calculate the “winning ratio” statistic I took the number of wins divided by the number of games and multiplied it my the number of games divided by the number of games divided by the number of games plus ten ((num_wins / num_games) * (num_games / (num_games + 10))). I do this to essentially penalize teams that played smaller amounts of games. I implemented this using the d3-geo-projection library and d3-topo-jason map dataset to draw out the map. Since the map data and the winning ratio data were separate, while creating the map I also had read in the latter csv to specify fill.

 

  1. Lastly, we are trying to bet on which team will win the world cup 2022. Over the last 2 world cups, which teams were top performing. You can decide how to interpret "top performing". A few approaches we would recommend: winning percentage in the world cup, victory strength, strength of opponent. You may show any combination of those. We don't have a specific answer we expect, and you should explain your choice in the written questions.

 

-I interpreted top performing the same way FIFA does itself, the Elo rating system. After each game points are added to or subtracted from a team's rating according to the formula: P = P_before + I(W-W_e) where P is equal to the current amount of points P_before is the team’s previous amount of points, I is the importance coefficient for the game (25 for FIFA World Cup qualification matches, 50 for FIFA World Cup matches before the quarter finals, and 60 for World Cup matches after the quarter finals). I aggregated the amount of points for each team in the dataset who partook in the past two world cups and qualification and then represented the point progression of each team in a separate connected scatter plot. The plot for the countries within the top ten amount of points can be shown when clicking the button with the country name denoted. The processing of data to get the aggregate points over time can be seen in the make_csv.py file.
